Hello there!

I am interested in building AI to help people explore the structure of knowledge and concepts across domains. Currently I am a graduate student at Carnegie Mellon University, where my research focuses on useful and general NLP methods for representation and reasoning. I am also passionate about libraries, and I contribute to computational social science research.

Previously, I worked on a startup at YCombinator, on autonomous driving at Google X, and on technology policy at Harvard. I received undergraduate degrees in computer science and philosophy from the University of Chicago.

Outside of computer science I have also been an actor, a mock trial competitor, and publicly angry about climate change. I enjoy cooking, making music, reading poetry, hiking, and going out dancing (global health permitting).
